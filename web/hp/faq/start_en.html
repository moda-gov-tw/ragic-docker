How do I delete a sheet or tab?

You can access the context menu of a tab or sheet by right-clicking on it, or by clicking on the arrow icon that appears when you hover on the tab or sheet.

Choosing "Delete" will delete the tab or sheet. If you've accidentally deleted a tab or sheet, check the Recycle Bin in your Account Setup. If you would like to recover lost data, you can contact support@ragic.com to help you to restore a backup.

How do I create great looking forms?

1. Add your company logo

It's always a great idea to customize Ragic to fit your company's look & feel. If you are using Ragic's professional or on-premise plan, you can replace the Ragic logo in the top-left to your company's logo. (if you're using a different plan, check the next tip!)

Go to your Company Setting under your Account Setup. You can upload your company logo on this page so that it'll show up on every page.


2. Add images to your form design

Adding a header or footer on paper or on digital forms usually works great to establish your corporate identity. Same goes for banners and your logo. The design elements you add here will be displayed in the printer-friendly version as well.

To add a default image that will always display on all your forms, simply add a static text field with an HTML image tag containing a link to your image. Learn how to do this by following the simple instructions here.


3. Mix and match your colors

You can change the style of static text fields as well as the headers of regular fields to fit your company's corporate identity. Whether the colors you choose match your logo or your website's color scheme, editing the colors will add a sense of integrity to your overall design.


4. Make the columns fit just right.

Whether it's your listing page or form page, having overset text is pretty unseemly. To avoid this, you can drag the edges of the column header from the design mode to fit your field header, or the width of your field values.

Additionally, you can change the field header text from the design mode of your listing page. The changes made for what is displayed on the listing page will not reflect to the form page, and vice versa.


5. What about the rows?

If you'd like to keep everything on one line, you can have a field span multiple columns from the Basic tab while designing your form page.

If you'd rather have a paragraph-like field that would expand as you enter more data, you can resize the height of the row, just like editing the width of the columns in the design mode.


6. Add some space

It's always a good idea to leave some white space for better readability, like how you'd leave a margin when composing a text document.

Sometimes a great way for this in Ragic is to leave an occasional empty column (a 50-px width looks great) between fields in the same row in the form page.


7. Keep your listing page clean.

One of the best features that comes with using Ragic is the powerful search engine — you can search for values that aren't displayed on the listing page with any of the search tools.

You can also click on individual entries to go to the form page and view the details of each entry.

This means that you don't have to display most of the fields on your listing page, save the important data. You can choose which fields are displayed as columns in the listing page from the design mode.

How to import from Excel or CSV into Ragic?

Ragic has a convenient import wizard for users to import from their existing data from Excel or CSV files. To import from your files to Ragic, you can go to the listing page that you would like to import your data to, and choose Import Data From File under Tools. There will be a step-by-step wizard to guide you through the field mapping process as well as other configurations for the data to import correctly.

If you're not importing into an existing Ragic sheet, you can also use the Excel or CSV file to create a new sheet as you import. Just choose "Create Form from Excel" instead in the "Tools" menu.

To learn more about each step in the import wizard, check our Knowledge Base entry.

Can I embed a sheet into my website or blog?

Ragic applications come with a variety of useful Web front ends to embed on your website or to be sent as a link separately. These Web plug-ins are available automatically as you create your sheet. Just click on the "Web Embed" button at the top right corner to see the Web plug-ins available.

Here you can choose from four types of plug-ins. After following the steps in the wizard, Ragic will give you an HTML code snippet to copy & paste, which you can use to embed the plug-in on your website. Or you can simply use the link provided for your users to directly use the plug-in as a standalone page.

One great advantage of using the Web plug-in is that you do not need to purchase user licenses for users of the Web plug-in. They will be registered as "External Users" to your account, which is free and unlimited. External users can freely use your Web plug-ins to enter or view data on your Ragic database, but cannot access the backend of the Ragic sheets that you created.

If a visitor who has not logged in to Ragic accesses a Web plug-in which does not allow unregistered users (you can configure the default user group "Everyone"), they will be redirected to a log-in screen for your account. These users can log-in or register for a free external user account there, and will be redirected to your Web plug-in afterwards.

You can check the external users registered for your application under External Users in your Account Setup, and you can also make modifications to the form so that you can ask for additional information from users as they register. This way, Ragic can act as a flexible and powerful way to manage your website members and content that they can access with multiple Web plug-ins embedded on different pages.

Can I store files, images, and videos on Ragic?

Yes, Ragic supports the upload of all types of files. In the Design Mode, you can choose the field type as File Upload or Image Upload.

Learn more about different field types here.

Another way to do this is to simply drag and drop the File Upload or Image Upload components from the "add component" tab at the left.

When a user is adding a new entry to the form, they will be prompted to upload an image or a file to the corresponding upload field. Files will be shown as a link to download the file, and images will be displayed as a thumbnail on the sheet. Clicking on the image will display the full-sized version of the image.

Can I save my data as Excel or print it out?

Yes, just go to "Tools" and choose "Download as Excel File". For printer friendly version, just choose "Printer Friendly" instead.

You can also save your data as a .csv file if you choose "Download as Text File".

This works both on listing page and form pages. If you download the listing page, you can choose to download the data on all entries regardless of the paging.

How do I create an HTML form that saves its data to a Ragic form?

This is very simple to do with Ragic if you know how to create forms in HTML. Suppose you want to create a form that saves entry in this sample pet store merchandise form.

1. Find the Field Id for each field that you would like to save in the HTML form. You can find them in the design mode when you focus on a field.

2. Create a form like this HTML sample, Your HTML form saves data to the same form URL, with one difference: change www.ragic.com to api.ragic.com. Put down the field id as the parameter name for each field to map them to a field on the Ragic form:

  <!-- Your HTML form saves data to the same form URL, except change www.ragic.com to api.ragic.com -->
  <!-- Adding the web parameter means that api server returns html instead of default JSON -->
  <form action="http://api.ragic.com/start/petstore/1?html" method="POST">

    <!-- Put down the field id as the parameter name for each field to map them to a field on the Ragic form -->
    <p>Item Id: <input type="text" name="1000001" /></p>
    <p>Item Name: <input type="text" name="1000003" /></p>
    <p>Item Price: <input type="text" name="1000005" /></p>

    <!-- Selections work the same way, just pass the selection value as parameter value -->
    <p>Item Category:
      <input type="radio" name="1000002" value="Dog" /> Dog
      <input type="radio" name="1000002" value="Cat" /> Cat
    </p>
  </form>

3. Make sure that the user has the correct access rights to enter data on this form.

How do I backup my data?

Ragic servers are backed up daily automatically. But if you need an extra layer of protection, you can manually back up your data by going to the Backup & Restore section in your Account Setup page.

Choose Download backup of your account, and download the backup.

If you want to download your data in a format that is easier to read, you can also use the Download as Excel File or Download as Text File feature that could be accessed in the listing page of a form under Tools.

How to let other people see the data on my sheet?

You can simply give out the URL of your database in the address bar of your browser to other people for them to access. All accounts, sheets and entries have their unique URL.

But for other people to actually see your data, they must have access rights to your sheet. You can set up the access right to your sheet in the Design Mode from the "change design", button. Choose the "access rights" tab under the "form setting" at the top left corner.

All of the user groups in your account will be listed here. One of the default groups named "Everyone" represents anyone who is not a user in your account, or for users who have not yet logged in.

You can select a role for each group of users. The access privilege of the role is specified in your user management sheet within your account setup. If a user is in multiple groups, the access right for a form would be the group that grants this user the most privilege.

Remember to save the form design after you change the access right setting. You do not need to set this for both listing page and form page, the access right is applied to all pages of a sheet.

If a user does not see the data after you have granted the necessary access rights, ask them to log out and log back in to refresh their access right settings.

What happens when two users try to update the same entry?

When a user begins editing an entry, they will create a lock for this entry's current state being edited. When another user simultaneously tries to edit this entry, Ragic will display a pop-up telling the other user that the entry is locked, and they should wait until the first user has finished editing to avoid overwriting each other's changes.

The entry will be unlocked when the editing user saves or cancels their edit, or goes to another page without saving. Ragic allows a user to manually unlock the lock that another user has created, in case they cannot find the editing user, and there is some urgent need to edit this entry. But in this scenario, they must understand the risk of overwriting each other's drafts, as the system will only save the changes of the last edit.

How do I add my team members to my database?

Navigate to your User Management sheet under your Account Setup, which can be found in the Start tab. This will take you to a list of all the users in your account. You can add a new user by clicking on the "+New" button.

When the new user's information is saved, Ragic will automatically send an e-mail to the new user containing their temporary password to log into the system. The user can change his password upon logging into Ragic.

Can some fields be visible only for certain group of users?

Yes. To do this you will need to create multiple versions of your sheet. You can configure your sheets to look different for different groups of users.

To create two versions of the same sheet, first right click on the sheet, or click on the little arrow when you hover on a sheet, and choose "Duplicate Sheet". Ragic will create a copy of your sheet. These two sheet will be pointing to the same set of entries in the database if you choose "use the same data source on new sheet". When you add a new entry in one sheet, it will also appear in the other. All edits and deletion will also be on both sheets.

Afterwards, you can configure the access rights for these two sheets, you can setup one sheet visible to one group, and the other sheet only visible to the other group of users.

After changing the access rights, you can go to the Design Mode for both sheets to add and remove the fields that are intended for different groups of users. This way, different groups of users will see different versions of the same sheet.

Can some entries be visible or editable only for certain group of users?

Yes, this can be done by setting up different users to play different roles in each sheets. If a user's role is Survey User, they can only view the entries they're the owner of, and if a user's role is Bulletin User, they can see all the entries, but can only edit the entries they are the owner of.

By default, a user is owner to only entries that he created, but by changing the input type for one of the fields in the form to Select User or Select Group and choosing Set selected user as entry owner, an owner of an entry can be added as the entry is created or edited. You can also change the owner of a field to a group, this way all the users in the group will be able to see or edit the entry as the privilege is assigned to them when this field is set.

I have a lot of users outside my company that use our DB once in a while, how can I save on user licenses?

There are some alternatives to handling a large number of external users.

1. Public sheets: You can set up the sheet so that everybody can access it without logging in with a Ragic account. If your application does not require access right management by users, you can simply set the access right from Everyone to Survey User so everyone can fill it out. Or to Bulletin Users so that everyone can not only fill it out, and also see other peoples' entries.

If the user needs to log back in the system later and look at the latest status of their entry or provide updates, take a look at the next approach "E-mail Id".

2. E-mail Id: Ragic has a convenient way of giving a user temporary access without having an account on Ragic. This can be done by setting an E-mail Id. attribute. When this attribute is set to a field in the form, the user will need to put down his e-mail in this field. As the form is saved, Ragic will automatically send an e-mail to this e-mail address. This e-mail will contain a link that can authenticate that the user is the owner of this e-mail as they use this link to log into Ragic.

Now that Ragic knows who this user is, even though they don't have a Ragic account, Ragic can determine which records they can see and which records can be edited by this certain user. This is usually used so that the user can see changes or edits to the entry that they create.

The shortcoming of this approach is that (1) the user will always need that e-mail to log in, (2) users cannot be managed in groups, therefore you won't be able to assign different privileges to them through Ragic's standard groups.

3. Volume Licensing: While Ragic is not a costly solution, if the number of user licenses needed is large, it can seem pretty expensive. Ragic understands this and offers volume license and site licenses for you to choose from. Please contact sales@ragic.com and describe your license use, so we can assist you on choosing the best plan for your organization.

What's the difference between Lite and Professional version?

1. The Lite version has a limit of 5 Ragic sheets whereas the professional version has no such limit. A Ragic sheet refers to one type of form for users to enter data. If you have a customer information sheet containing data on 100 clients, it would still only be counted as 1 sheet, since the forms can have unlimited entries.

2. The Lite version does not support server-side Javascript customization. In the professional version, all forms can trigger our server-side Javascript scripting engine to run complex business logic. Basically any business logic that you would like to run, but cannot be covered by Ragic's existing functions, can be covered by server-side scripting.

3. You can only have one system administrator that can access the design and backend in the Lite version while the professional version has no limit.

For more information, see our pricing page, or contact sales@ragic.com.

How do I upgrade or start my subscription?

Click on the "Upgrade Account" button at the top right corner of the page to proceed with payment and start your subscription. Choose your Ragic plan from the Pricing page.

You will be redirected to a secure credit card transaction system for payment.

You can also upgrade or change plans any time by going to the Account Setup page and choose Account Billing.

Can I change my plan?

Yes, you can change your plan anytime you want from your Account Setup page under Account Billing.

What happens if I reach storage limit?

Most of our limits are "soft limits", meaning that your application will not stop functioning when the limit is reached. We will simply notify you that you must upgrade your account to accommodate your current usage.
